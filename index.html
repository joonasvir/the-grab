Methodology

How the number is built

The headline figure tracks documented, direct revenue and gifts flowing to Donald Trump, his family, or entities they control since the second inauguration on January 20, 2025. It is anchored on the running tally maintained by the Center for American Progress’ “Trump’s Take” project1 and cross-checked against reporting by The New Yorker, Forbes, the Brennan Center, the House Judiciary Committee minority staff, and CREW.2345

The counter is not Trump’s net-worth change — Forbes pegs that at roughly +$3–4 billion since January 2025, driven mostly by unrealized paper gains on his crypto holdings and his Trump Media (DJT) stake.26

Where dated lump sums exist (a legal settlement, an accepted foreign gift), the ledger adds them on the date they were finalized. Where revenue is ongoing (token sales, stablecoin reserve interest, branding fees), it is amortized as a per-second accrual rate derived from the most recent publicly reported total divided by the elapsed time. The per-second figure is therefore a smoothed average, not a real bank-account flow.

The number is conservative. It excludes: the ~$5 billion paper value of Trump-family WLFI tokens that have not yet been sold;4 the increased Mar-a-Lago and Bedminster membership fees;11 Secret Service payments to Trump properties; the $239 million inauguration fund;12 and the multi-billion-dollar announced (but not yet realized) Trump-Organization real-estate pipeline in Saudi Arabia, the UAE, Oman, and Qatar.78
